Solution Overview & Benefits

The Oxfam-DelAgua Single Incubator Kit is designed for microbiological water monitoring via detection of indicator organisms (fecal coliforms) for E. coli, cholera, salmonella and other fecal water-borne pathogens. The kit allows for complete portability for testing in remote areas, and it has an internal battery, sealed within the case housing, which can be re-charged from power mains or a car battery.

The kit is designed to carry out six basic tests to measure the quality of drinking water: -microbiological quality -turbidity -free chlorine -total chlorine -pH -temperature

Users require the following items to start testing: -Pressure cooker, portable sterilizer or access to an autoclave e.g. in a hospital. -Methanol (approx. 1 ml per-test) -Distilled water -1 liter measuring cylinder or beaker -Clean cloths

History & Development

The Oxfam-DelAgua Single Incubator Kit is primarily used in the field, but may also be used in a laboratory or other permanent locations. Built with an internal battery, it is capable of up to five incubation cycles between recharges, making it completely independent for a full week of off-site surveying.

The kit was designed to help provide information about the safety of water supplies in difficult situations where laboratory facilities do not exist.
